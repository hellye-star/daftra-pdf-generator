Audience & Engagement Insight
📉
Why interaction may be low on certain posts
Static image posts receive 60% less reach than Reels on average, because Instagram's algorithm deprioritises non-video formats in the Explore feed. If a post has high reach but low saves and shares, the issue is likely weak content hook — not posting time. If reach itself is low, the issue is format or hashtag relevance.
🎯
Why the Before & After Reel attracted more profile visits
Transformation content (before/after, process reveal, client result) creates strong curiosity and immediate relevance for potential clients. Viewers who see a result they want are significantly more likely to visit the profile to explore more work. This format also drives saves, which signals the algorithm to show the post to a broader audience over time.
💬
Low comments — possible weak call-to-action
Only 8.3% of posts this period had a clear question or CTA in the caption. Posts with a direct question in the caption average 2.4× more comments. Adding a simple "Which version do you prefer?" or "Tag a brand that needs this" at the end of the caption costs nothing and significantly improves comment rate.

Performance Summary — Week of 2 Jun 2026
Cross-channel analysis across Instagram 1, Instagram 2, TikTok, and Google Ads · All figures are sample mock data
01
Why engagement is low on certain posts — and what to fix
Across both Instagram accounts, static image posts average 1.5–2.1% engagement, while Reels average 4.8–5.8%. The issue is not posting time, hashtags, or frequency — it is content format. Instagram's algorithm gives Reels 3–4× more distribution to non-followers. Posts with no question or CTA in the caption average 60% fewer comments than those that do. Low saves on IG2 specifically suggest the content is not creating a "save for later" moment — educational posts, process breakdowns, and client result reveals are the strongest drivers of saves.

02
Why the Before & After Reel attracted more profile visits — and how to replicate it
The "Brand Identity Showcase — Before & After" Reel on Instagram 1 generated 420 profile visits — 3× the account average. Transformation content works because it demonstrates value visually in under 30 seconds, creates immediate relevance for potential clients who recognise their own problem, and drives saves which extend the post's lifespan in the algorithm. The key elements that made it work: (1) the hook was the visual contrast in the first frame, not text, (2) it showed a real client result, not a generic example, (3) it was under 35 seconds. Replicate this format with 2–3 more client results over the next 4 weeks.

03
Content type performance this period — Reels > Carousels > Static > Stories
Reels are the top-performing format on both Instagram accounts by every metric: reach, engagement rate, saves, profile visits, and leads. Carousels perform well for saves and are effective for portfolio and educational content. Static posts underperform consistently — the main issue is limited algorithm distribution, not content quality. Stories generate link taps and replies but limited new discovery. The clearest action is reducing static posts to 1 per week maximum and replacing them with short Reels (under 35 seconds) or educational carousels.

04
The Google Ads conversion problem is search intent mismatch, not budget
The "graphic design" broad-match keyword has 920 clicks and zero conversions. This is a classic intent mismatch: people searching "graphic design" are looking for tutorials, tools, and inspiration — not to hire a studio. The click-through rate (9%) is also significantly lower than the best keyword (31%), confirming the traffic is low-quality. Pausing this keyword and reallocating its sample budget to "brand identity design jeddah" or similar phrase-match terms would likely double conversions without increasing total spend.

TikTok has the lowest sample CPL — consider shifting budget from Instagram 2
TikTok delivered a sample cost per lead of SAR 43, compared to SAR 55 on Instagram 2 and SAR 47 on Instagram 1. Engagement rate (5.1%) is also the highest across all paid channels. The short-video format on TikTok rewards the same content types that work on Instagram Reels, so existing content can be cross-posted with minimal adaptation. Shifting 15–20% of the Instagram 2 ad budget to TikTok for 2 weeks and comparing results would provide a meaningful real-data comparison.
